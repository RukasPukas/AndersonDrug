Richmond Agitation-Sedation Scale

Score Term Description ECIEMS Treatment
+4 Combative Overtly combative, violent, immediate danger to staff Midazolam / Haloperidol
- OR -
Ketamine**
+3 Very Agitated Pulls or removes tubes or catheters, aggressive behavior Midazolam / Haloperidol
+2 Agitated Frequent non-purposeful movement, fights interventions Midazolam / Haloperidol
+1 Restless Anxious but movements are not aggressive or vigorous Verbal assurance and calm patient
0 Alert and Calm Normal behavior, no action required No action required
-1 Drowsy Not fully alert, but has sustained awakening and eye contact to voice (More than 10 seconds)
-2 Light Sedation Briefly awakens with eye contact to voice (Less than 10 seconds)
-3 Moderate Sedation Movement or eye opening to voice (no eye contact)
-4 Deep Sedation No response to voice but movement or eye opening to physical stimulation
-5 Unarousable No response to voice or physical stimulation

** If Ketamine is used per this guideline, the EMS system office shall be notified within 24 hours for QA
