EKG Placement
Regular, Right-Sided, Posterior

Regular EKG Placement

Indications:

• Suspected cardiac patient (CHF, pulmonary edema, dysrhythmias, palpitations)
• Suspected Acute Coronary Syndrome (chest, jaw, arm, epigastric discomfort, etc.)
• Suspected tricyclic overdose
• Electrical injuries
• Syncope
• Shortness of breath

Posterior EKG Placement

Indications:

• Suspected cardiac patient
• Reciprocal changes in leads V1-V3.

Posterior MI is suggested by the following changes:
• Horizontal ST depression
• Tall, broad R waves (>30 ms)
• Upright T waves
• Dominant R wave (R/S ratio >1) in V2

Right-Sided EKG Placement

Indications:

• Suspected cardiac patient
• Inferior STEMI patients with ST elevation in V1 and ST elevation in lead III > lead II
• Isoelectric ST segment in V1 with marked ST depression in V2
